What is Porchfest?

Every porch is a stage.

Porchfest started when a few Carriage Town residents put bands on their front porches and invited the neighborhood. That was seven years ago. Now it fills six blocks, draws thousands of people, and has become one of Genesee County's most popular festivals.

You walk through a neighborhood and the music finds you. Porches, street corners, front yards. Turn any direction and something is happening.

The festival spreads across residential porches, sidewalk stages, and the main stage at Mason Street and First Avenue. Food vendors line the blocks. The whole thing runs from afternoon into the evening, and every part of it is free.

We prioritize Flint-based artists and acts connected to the community. All genres, all forms of performance. If you play music, serve food, or bring something that makes a block party better, there might be a porch with your name on it.

How Porchfest Works

Show up. Walk around. Find your sound.

Free entry, open streets, live music everywhere. Just get here on July 17th and start walking.

01

Show up

Carriage Town neighborhood, Friday July 17th, starting in the afternoon. Street parking is free. The festival covers about six blocks centered on Mason Street and First Avenue.

02

Walk the blocks

Every porch and street corner has a different act. Hip-hop next to folk next to a DJ next to a comedian. Just keep walking and the lineup changes every 50 feet.

03

Stay all day

Grab food from any vendor on Mason Street. Find a lawn chair spot. Bring the family. The festival runs until the sun goes down and sometimes a little longer.
